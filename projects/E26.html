Wind Turbine Design

Designed and analyzed a small-scale horizontal-axis wind turbine focused on structural stability, aerodynamic efficiency, and experimental performance validation under constrained design requirements.

Project Overview

This project involved designing and fabricating a compact wind turbine system capable of converting airflow into electrical power under strict geometric and manufacturing constraints. The system included a 3D-printed rotor, structural tower, and generator housing designed using SolidWorks.

Key Design Constraints

  • Limited 3D printing volume and material constraints
  • Height restriction: 14.25 in (vs. 16 in target requirement)
  • Structural stability under wind loading and vibration
  • Maximize electrical output efficiency

Engineering Approach

The turbine used a three-blade horizontal-axis configuration with a 4.5° blade pitch angle. Structural design emphasized stiffness and load distribution through a reinforced two-level tower geometry. Finite Element Analysis (SolidWorks) was used to evaluate stress, displacement, and safety factors prior to fabrication.

Performance Results

Power Output 0.061 W
Tower Stiffness 0.6405 lbf/mm
Max Deflection 1.41 mm
Wind Condition ~28 mph airflow

The system successfully generated electrical power, though performance was limited by reduced tower height and alignment sensitivity affecting rotor exposure to airflow.

Key Engineering Insights

  • Blade geometry had a dominant effect on rotational efficiency and power output
  • Structural stiffness strongly depended on tower reinforcement layout
  • FEA trends matched experimental behavior but deviated due to real-world setup errors
  • System performance was highly sensitive to small geometric deviations

Conclusion

This project demonstrated a complete engineering workflow including conceptual design, CAD modeling, simulation, fabrication, and experimental testing. While target performance was not fully achieved, the system validated core aerodynamic and structural design principles in a constrained engineering environment.
