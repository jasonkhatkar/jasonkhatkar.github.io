E29 Group 6 - Multi-Function Phone Case System

A multifunctional phone case system designed to integrate protective casing with embedded utility tools while balancing manufacturability, material constraints, and fit precision.

Project Overview

This project focused on designing a modular phone case system consisting of multiple functional layers, including an inner phone case, outer protective structure, and integrated multi-tool component. The design required careful material selection, tolerance control, and manufacturability analysis across multiple fabrication methods.

Design Objectives

  • Create a functional protective phone case system
  • Integrate utility tools (knife, firestarter, screwdriver, bottle opener)
  • Ensure manufacturability using available lab resources
  • Balance flexibility, durability, and precision

Material & Process Selection

Multiple material-process combinations were evaluated based on flexibility, durability, tolerance capability, and manufacturability constraints.

  • TPU (NinjaFlex) - 3D Printed: Selected for inner phone case due to flexibility, adjustability, and ease of iteration.
  • PLA - 3D Printed: Used for prototype components due to ease of printing and fast iteration.
  • Steel - Fablight machining: Selected for tool components requiring high durability, hardness, and tight tolerances.

Tradeoffs included flexibility vs. durability, tolerance precision vs. manufacturability time, and material rigidity vs. usability.

Fit & Tolerancing

The system required multiple precision fits including rotational joints and interference fits to ensure structural stability and usability.

  • Rotational joints used RT6 tolerance classes
  • Interference fits used for structural alignment between layers
  • Critical dimensions ranged from ~0.05 mm to ~0.08 mm tolerances

Manufacturing feasibility analysis confirmed that selected processes were capable of achieving required tolerances.

Key Engineering Considerations

  • Material flexibility vs structural rigidity tradeoffs
  • Multi-process manufacturing coordination
  • Precision alignment between layered components
  • User ergonomics and usability constraints
