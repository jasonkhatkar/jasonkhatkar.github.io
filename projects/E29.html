Project Overview

Developed a modular phone case system consisting of an inner flexible case, outer protective shell, and integrated multi-tool component. The system required coordination across multiple manufacturing methods while maintaining precise fit between interfacing components.

Focus: multi-material design, tight tolerancing, and manufacturability under constrained fabrication methods.

Design Objectives

  • Develop a functional protective phone case system with integrated utility tools
  • Ensure manufacturability using available lab processes (3D printing + machining)
  • Balance durability, flexibility, and user ergonomics
  • Maintain precise fit across multi-layer assembly

Materials & Manufacturing Strategy

Material selection was driven by tradeoffs between flexibility, strength, and fabrication constraints.

  • TPU (NinjaFlex) — inner case for flexibility and impact absorption
  • PLA — rapid iteration prototypes for form and fit testing
  • Steel (Fablight machining) — high-strength tool components requiring tight tolerances
Key Tradeoff: flexibility vs structural rigidity vs manufacturability time.

Fit, Assembly & Tolerancing

Designed multi-part assembly requiring controlled fits between layers, including rotational joints and interference fits.

  • RT6 tolerance class applied for rotational interfaces
  • Interference fits used for structural alignment and retention
  • Critical dimensions controlled within ~0.05–0.08 mm range
Manufacturing feasibility confirmed across additive and subtractive processes for required tolerances.

Key Engineering Considerations

  • Multi-process manufacturing coordination (3D printing + machining)
  • Mechanical interface alignment across layered assembly
  • Design for manufacturability under lab constraints
  • User ergonomics and functional usability integration

Deliverables

  • Final CAD assemblies and iterative design revisions
  • Manufacturing and process selection analysis
  • Tolerancing and fit validation documentation
  • Prototype evaluation and refinement cycles
