Roto-Peel

Cable-driven rotating pizza peel mechanism designed to improve heat uniformity during oven baking by enabling controlled in-oven rotation.

ME126/226L Mechanical Design Project · Product Development · Prototyping

Overview

The Roto-Peel is a hybrid kitchen tool combining a traditional pizza peel with a cable-driven rotational mechanism, allowing controlled rotation of pizza inside an oven without repositioning or removal.

Problem

Uneven heating in conventional ovens leads to inconsistent pizza cooking due to spatial temperature gradients and convection effects. Manual rotation is imprecise, inconvenient, and interrupts thermal stability.

Solution

We developed a cable-actuated rotational system integrated into a pizza peel. Pulling a cable generates offset torque that rotates the peel surface smoothly about a central axis while maintaining stability inside the oven.

Engineering Design

Mechanism

Cable-driven actuation replaces a crank system to reduce geometric constraints. The system converts linear pull force into rotational motion through offset torque applied at the rotation axis.

Structure & Fabrication

Designed using a hybrid approach combining machined metal components for load paths with 3D-printed housings for rapid iteration. Bearing-supported rotation enabled smooth motion, while an elastic return system provided reset functionality.

Testing & Results

The prototype successfully demonstrated controlled rotation of loaded pizza in a constrained oven environment. The system maintained smooth actuation under repeated use, validating feasibility of cable-driven rotational control in kitchen applications.

Limitations Observed

  • PLA components showed reduced durability under repeated thermal/mechanical load
  • Cable routing introduced friction losses and reduced efficiency
  • Return mechanism required tuning for consistent reset behavior

Key Improvements for Future Iterations

  • Replace polymer components with heat-resistant materials (metal/wood)
  • Optimize cable routing to reduce friction and slack
  • Improve rotational range control for better thermal uniformity
  • Transition toward manufacturable, production-ready design
