DeskLift

Motorized ergonomic desk platform using a four-bar linkage system (ME130 Mechanical Design Project)

Project Overview

DeskLift is a motorized mechanical system designed to improve ergonomic workspace efficiency by enabling dynamic repositioning of a desk surface. The system allows a user to switch between a flat workspace and an elevated platform for improved accessibility and organization.

Designed a motor-driven four-bar linkage system achieving up to ~95° motion with stable toggle behavior and rapid actuation.

Engineering Approach

Mechanical System

  • Four-bar linkage with toggle positions
  • Plywood frame with aluminum reinforcement
  • Gear reduction (4:1) for torque amplification
  • Designed for stable motion under load

Controls & Electronics

  • ESP32 microcontroller system
  • DC geared motor with encoder feedback
  • Push-button actuation control
  • Integrated power supply module

Key Performance Metrics

  • Load capacity: ~5–8 lbs
  • Motion range: 0°–95°
  • Actuation time: ~1–4 seconds
  • Maximum structural deflection: ~0.5 mm (worst case analysis)
  • Maximum stress: ~44 MPa (below 57.9 MPa material yield)

Analysis & Validation

The system was validated through both kinematic modeling and structural analysis. Motion behavior was tuned to ensure stable transition between two toggle positions, balancing speed with mechanical loading during acceleration phases.

Structural evaluation assumed a ~1.5 kg applied load, with results confirming safe operation below material yield limits under worst-case loading conditions.

Key Challenges

  • Managing dynamic loads during rapid motion transitions
  • Calibration differences between unloaded and loaded motor behavior
  • Achieving reliable motion without complex sensing systems

Outcome

DeskLift demonstrates a compact electromechanical system integrating structural design, actuation, and control. The final prototype achieved stable lifting motion with repeatable toggle behavior and validated structural safety margins.
