Airfoil Lift Characterization

Experimental investigation of lift behavior for an unknown airfoil using wind tunnel testing, focusing on the relationship between lift coefficient, angle of attack, and Reynolds number.

Objective

Characterize aerodynamic lift behavior of an unknown airfoil by measuring lift forces across varying angles of attack and flow velocities, and compute lift coefficients under different Reynolds numbers.

Approach

  • Wind tunnel testing across multiple flow speeds
  • Angle of attack sweep: -16° to +16°
  • Lift measured using calibrated strain gauge system
  • Velocity measured via pitot tube + anemometer cross-validation

Key Methods

Sensor outputs were calibrated using regression models to convert voltage signals into physical quantities (force, pressure, velocity). Uncertainty propagation accounted for measurement noise, calibration error, and geometric tolerances.

Results

  • Linear lift response at small angles of attack
  • Clear stall behavior at higher angles
  • Symmetric lift curve for ± angles
  • Consistent behavior across Reynolds numbers (~69k–160k)
  • Strong agreement between velocity measurement methods

Engineering Insight

The airfoil exhibited symmetric lift characteristics consistent with theoretical expectations for symmetric profiles at low Reynolds numbers. Despite wind tunnel limitations, experimental results closely matched predicted aerodynamic trends, validating both measurement approach and calibration methods.
